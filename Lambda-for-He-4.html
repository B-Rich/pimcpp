In our units of Kelvin and Angstrom (and defining \(\hbar=1\)), the appropriate value for He-4 is \(\lambda = 6.059615\).

If these units are unfamiliar to you, you should check this is the correct value for \(\lambda=\frac{\hbar^2}{2m}\).

Starting with SI units, for example, we have
\(\hbar=1.0545e-34~J\cdot s\)
\(m = 4 m_p = 6.68e-27~kg\)
which gives \(\lambda=8.32313e-43~J\cdot m^2\).

Inserting conversion factors of \(10^{10}\frac{\AA}{m}\) and \(1.38e-23~\frac{J}{K}\), we’re left with \(\lambda=6.059615~K\cdot\AA^2\).

Equivalently, in this system of units, the proton mass is \(m_p = 0.0207~K^{-1}\AA^{-2}\).
